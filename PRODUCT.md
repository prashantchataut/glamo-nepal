# GLAMO Nepal — Product Context

## Product

GLAMO Nepal is a premium beauty and cosmetics e-commerce platform serving customers in Nepal. The site sells authentic skincare, makeup, haircare, and body care products with NPR pricing and local delivery across Nepal, primarily Kathmandu Valley.

## Users

- **Primary**: Women aged 22-40 in Kathmandu Valley and Pokhara, shopping for daily beauty routines and gifting. They value authenticity, clear pricing in NPR, and reliable delivery information.
- **Secondary**: Men aged 25-45 buying gifts for partners or family. They want quick decisions, clear product recommendations, and hassle-free checkout.
- **Tertiary**: Beauty professionals and salon owners ordering in bulk, who need product details, ingredient lists, and brand authenticity verification.

## Brand Voice

- **Warm but not saccharine.** Confident, editorial, and direct. Like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know skincare, not a salesperson.
- **Nepal-aware.** References to local routines, climate, festivals, and delivery realities are natural, not forced.
- **Clear over clever.** Product descriptions, pricing, and delivery timelines are straightforward. No jargon, no vague promises.
- **Restraint with personality.** The site feels curated and calm, not overwhelming. White space is intentional. Every section earns its place.

## Anti-References

- **Not**: Sephora's maximalist product grid, Nykaa's promotional density, generic Shopify beauty templates with gradient hero sections and trust icon badges.
- **Not**: AI-generated landing pages with tiny uppercase labels above every heading, numbered benefit lists (01/02/03), floating product cards over hero images, or sparkle/bolt icons next to feature descriptions.
- **Not**: Clinical minimalist sites that feel cold or sterile. This is beauty, not pharmacy.

## Strategic Principles

1. **Curated, not overwhelming.** Fewer products per row, more whitespace, editorial selections over infinite scroll.
2. **NPR-first.** All pricing in Nepali Rupees. No USD conversions, no ambiguity. Delivery fees and timelines shown before checkout.
3. **Authenticity as trust signal.** Product pages show brand, origin, ingredients, and authenticity verification. No fake reviews or inflated ratings.
4. **Routine-oriented.** Products are organized by concern and routine (morning, evening, festival) rather than just category. The "Routines" section is a primary nav item.
5. **Mobile-first Nepal.** The majority of traffic is mobile on mid-range Android devices. Performance, touch targets, and data usage matter more than desktop animations.

## Register

**brand** — Design IS the product. GLAMO is a beauty brand site where visual identity, editorial feel, and emotional resonance are the deliverable. Every surface should feel considered and intentional, like a curated magazine rather than a product catalog.
